# Experiment H: Batched Parallelization of DMCI  (manuscript §4.5 `sec:exp_h`, Appendix `app:exp_h_ext`)

## What this shows
Because a fixed program's control flow and heap layout are data-independent, a leading
batch dimension broadcasts through the entire compiled interpreter graph unchanged, so the
~14x per-evaluation interpretation overhead is paid once per *batch* instead of once per
input. The experiment quantifies this: batched evaluation is bit-for-bit identical to
sequential (max diff < 1e-6, all gradients finite), yields forward throughput speedups of
279--1,710x at batch size 512, training speedups of 12--45x, population-batching speedups
of 9,156--16,873x at M=100, and on two production-scale simulators reaches 852--875x
throughput at batch size 1024 and a 3,848x end-to-end population-optimization speedup. It
is also benchmarked honestly against `jax.vmap` of the directly-compiled model (geometric
mean 0.27x forward), establishing that the within-system numbers are *recoveries of
interpreter overhead*, while DMCI's real advantage is zero per-program vectorization and
coverage of loop-heavy programs that the JAX backend cannot `jit`/`vmap` at all.

## Files
- `config.py` -- `ExpHConfig`: batch sizes [1..4096], population sizes [1,10,100,1000], 64 data points, 500 epochs, lr 0.01, the 12 heap-free batchable models from Experiment B (M01--M08, M10--M12, M14).
- `exp_h.py` -- main driver for Parts A--E on the 12 Experiment-B models. Part A forward throughput, Part B training speedup, Part C correctness, Part D population batching, Part E `torch.compile`. Writes `results/exp_h_{cpu,cuda}.json`. CLI: `--part A|B|C|D|E|all --device cpu|cuda`.
- `bench_diffesm.py` -- DiffESM-S large-model benchmark (Part F): 97-node Earth System Model, compiles `large_examples/diffesm_s.scm`, sequential vs batched (BS 1--1024) x timesteps {10,50,100}, CPU+GPU. Writes `results/bench_diffesm_{device}.json`.
- `bench_diffsoc.py` -- DiffSoc-S large-model benchmark (Part H): 206-node Urban Political Economy Simulator, compiles `large_examples/diffsoc_s.scm`, same sweep as DiffESM. Reuses `DEFAULT_PARAMS`/`PARAMS` from `test_diffsoc.py`. Writes `results/bench_diffsoc_{device}.json`.
- `test_diffsoc.py` -- harness + parameter/initial-state definitions for DiffSoc-S (compile, forward, backward, gradient check); imported by `bench_diffsoc.py`.
- `bench_convergence.py` -- Part G convergence speedup on DiffESM-S: fits 15 climate params to synthetic targets (N=32, 20 timesteps, 300 epochs, Adam lr 0.003, seed 42), comparing sequential / batched / population (M in {1,10,50,200}). Writes `results/convergence_{device}.json`.
- `bench_vmap.py` -- cross-system reference: DMCI `evaluate_batched` vs `jax.jit(jax.vmap(...))` of the *directly-compiled* graph emitted via `emit_jax_module`, on identical graph/data/CPU, with a numerical-equivalence guard. Writes `results/vmap_results.json`. (Backs Table `tab:exp_h_vmap`.)
- `bench_dmci_batched.py` -- batches the heap-using meta-circular interpreter *itself* (the case Parts A--E exclude), on 3 DMCI programs (Beer--Lambert equation, C03 decay chain, I_gpp 2-PFT). Writes `results/dmci_batched_bench.json`. (Backs the "214--270x forward, 56--63x training" claim.)
- `aggregate_table.py` -- reconstructs every cell of the 11 Experiment-H manuscript tables from the committed results and self-checks each against values transcribed from the manuscript (275/275 cells match). Run with no args.
- `slurm_submit.sh` -- A100 (n124/borowiec): Parts C, A (GPU+CPU), B, D -> `exp_h_{cpu,cuda}.json`.
- `slurm_part_e.sh` -- A100: Part E `torch.compile` GPU+CPU (overwrites `exp_h_cuda.json` with `{part_e}`; see Provenance note).
- `slurm_diffesm.sh` / `slurm_diffsoc.sh` -- A100: large-model benchmarks (CPU+GPU) -> `bench_diff{esm,soc}_all.json`.
- `slurm_convergence.sh` -- A100 node: Part G convergence (CPU) -> `convergence_cpu.json`.
- `slurm_vmap.sh` -- CPU `eight` partition: vmap baseline (jax[cpu]) -> `vmap_results.json`.
- `__init__.py` -- package marker.

## Data
- **Inputs:** Fully synthetic, no external dataset.
  - Parts A--E: the 12 heap-free models from `experiments/exp_b/models.py` (closed-form/program-tier scientific equations); inputs are `torch.linspace` over each model's `x_range`, targets are each model's analytic `ground_truth`. Deterministic (no RNG) for the throughput/correctness parts; Part D draws population inits from `torch.randn` (no fixed seed in `exp_h.py`, so Part-D population *timings* are reproducible but the exact random inits are not pinned).
  - Parts F/G/H: the two simulators are source programs committed in the repo: `large_examples/diffesm_s.scm` (302 lines, 97 nodes, 70 params) and `large_examples/diffsoc_s.scm` (688 lines, 206 nodes, 87 params). Parameters/initial state are hard-coded in `bench_diffesm.py`, `bench_convergence.py`, and `test_diffsoc.py`. Part G targets are self-generated by the model and the optimizer is seeded (`SEED=42`).
  - vmap baseline: same 12 models; population inits seeded via `np.random.default_rng(0)`.
- **Outputs (in `results/`):**
  - `exp_h_cuda.json` -- `{part_e: [...]}` (Part E `torch.compile`, A100). `exp_h_cpu.json` -- `{part_d: [...]}` (a separate CPU population run, *not* the table source; see note). Parts A/B/D table values come from the SLURM stdout `exp_h_5147138.out`.
  - `bench_diffesm_all.json` (72 records), `bench_diffsoc_all.json` (72 records) -- per (method, device, n_steps, batch_size) fwd/fwd+bwd times, per-eval ms, peak GPU MB, speedups.
  - `convergence_cpu.json` -- per-epoch (wall_time, loss/best_loss/mean_loss) trajectories for sequential, batched, and population M in {1,10,50,200}.
  - `vmap_results.json` -- per-model DMCI-vs-vmap fractions for forward / population / training, with `max_abs_diff` equivalence guard.
  - `dmci_batched_bench.json` -- per-program correctness (max diff, grads_finite) + forward/training speedups for the interpreter-itself batching.
  - `*.out` / `*.err` -- raw SLURM stdout/stderr for each job (`5147138`=Parts A-E, `5147148`=DiffESM, `5147165`=convergence, `5147177`/`5147179`=DiffSoc, `5147141`=Part E).
- **Provenance:** `results/MANIFEST.txt` lists all 19 committed files; verify with `python3 -m experiments.check_manifests` (reports OK, 19 files). JSON results are committed via Git LFS (see repo `.gitattributes`); on a fresh clone run `git lfs pull` before inspecting contents. Note: `slurm_part_e.sh` writes Part E into `exp_h_cuda.json`, overwriting the Part-D GPU JSON that originally lived there, so the Part-D manuscript table (147x..16,873x, A100) is recovered from `exp_h_5147138.out`, not from a JSON. `aggregate_table.py` documents this and machine-verifies the table value against the parsed log line.

## How to run
```
# On HPC (fortyfive.hpc.uidaho.edu); activate the project .venv first.
sbatch experiments/exp_h/slurm_submit.sh        # Parts A-E (A100)      -> exp_h_{cpu,cuda}.json
sbatch experiments/exp_h/slurm_part_e.sh        # Part E torch.compile  -> exp_h_cuda.json[part_e]
sbatch experiments/exp_h/slurm_diffesm.sh       # Part F DiffESM-S       -> bench_diffesm_all.json
sbatch experiments/exp_h/slurm_diffsoc.sh       # Part H DiffSoc-S       -> bench_diffsoc_all.json
sbatch experiments/exp_h/slurm_convergence.sh   # Part G convergence     -> convergence_cpu.json
sbatch experiments/exp_h/slurm_vmap.sh          # vmap baseline (eight)  -> vmap_results.json

# Interpreter-itself batching (CPU 'eight' partition is fine):
python3 -m experiments.exp_h.bench_dmci_batched --max-batch 1024 --train-epochs 20

# Fast local correctness smoke test (no GPU needed; ~1 min, overwrites exp_h_cpu.json):
python3 -m experiments.exp_h.exp_h --part C --device cpu

# Reconstruct + self-check every manuscript table against the committed results:
python3 -m experiments.exp_h.aggregate_table       # prints "SELF-CHECK ... 275/275 cells match"
```

## Expected results
- **Part C correctness** (Table `tab:exp_h_correctness`): all 12 models pass, max pred diff < 1e-6 (observed 0.0), loss at true params < 1e-13, all gradients finite.
- **Part A throughput** (Table `tab:exp_h_throughput`, A100): 279x (M08 Euler ODE) to 1,710x (M02 Beer--Lambert) at batch size 512.
- **Part B training** (Table `tab:exp_h_training`): 12--45x faster batched than sequential, losses matching.
- **Part D population** (Table `tab:exp_h_population`, A100): 9,156--16,873x at M=100.
- **vmap baseline** (Table `tab:exp_h_vmap`): geometric mean DMCI = 0.27x of `jax.vmap` forward; closed-form 0.36--0.93x, loop-heavy ~0.04--0.10x; training ~0.03--0.11x; equivalence guard max diff 9.5e-7.
- **Interpreter-itself batching:** 214--270x forward, 56--63x training (bit-for-bit, max diff 0.0).
- **Parts F/H cross-model** (Tables `tab:exp_h_crossmodel`, `tab:exp_h_diffesm`, `tab:exp_h_diffsoc`, `tab:exp_h_diffsoc_scaling`): both simulators 62--65x forward at BS=64 (DiffESM-S 61.6x, DiffSoc-S 64.6x) and 852--875x at BS=1024 (CPU, 100 steps); CPU outperforms A100 throughout.
- **Part G convergence** (Table `tab:convergence`): 300 epochs DiffESM-S = 23.9s batched vs 731.4s sequential (30.6x, identical loss 2.62e-9); M=200 population = 38.0s vs estimated 40.6h sequential = **3,848x** end-to-end, all 200 restarts converged.
- These populate the abstract/intro headline (875x throughput, 3,848x population speedup) and §4.5 + Appendix `app:exp_h_ext` Tables/Figures listed above.

## Environment, seeds, hardware
- **Python** 3.11.10; **PyTorch** 2.12.0+cu130 (CUDA 13.0); **JAX** (jax[cpu]) for the vmap baseline only.
- **Hardware:** GPU runs on a single NVIDIA A100-PCIE-40GB (HPC node n124, `borowiec` partition); CPU runs pinned to 1 thread (`torch.set_num_threads(1)`); vmap baseline on the `eight` CPU partition. The DiffESM/DiffSoc benchmarks need `sys.setrecursionlimit(5000--10000)` (set in-script) for the deep recursive integration.
- **Seeds:** Part G `SEED=42` (perturbation + population draws derived from it); vmap baseline population `default_rng(0)`; `bench_dmci_batched` uses fixed generator seeds (0/7) and `torch.manual_seed(0)` for param init. Parts A--E throughput/correctness are deterministic; Part D population inits in `exp_h.py` are *not* seeded (timings reproducible, exact inits not pinned).
- **Wall-clock (approx):** Parts A--E ~1--2 h on A100; each large-model benchmark ~1--3 h; convergence ~1 h; vmap baseline < 1 h on CPU. The local Part-C smoke test is ~1 min on CPU.
